Schrodinger's Wave Equation

Schrodinger's Wave Equation is a second-order linear differential equation that is useful for describing the motion of electrons through a crystal structure. Here's what we know:

  • The wave equation parallels the well-known equivalence of total energy to the sum of kinetic and potential energy (TE = KE + PE).
  • This also holds true for a given field described by the wave function ψ(x,t). Therefore, TEψ = KEψ + PEψ.
